Oscar Castellino is an international opera singer and the composer of the Mars anthem, Rise to Mars. Labeled as a "rising star in the operatic world" - BBC News, he has played more than 30 operatic roles in the UK, India and around the world.

Born in a car on a busy street in Mumbai, he traces his roots to Goa. Originally a software engineer, he was discovered for his operatic talent by Patricia Rozario during her concert tour of India. He then received a scholarship to study at the Royal College of Music and was subsequently selected to sing for the late Queen's Diamond Jubilee at the River Thames.

Oscar sings in a variety of European and Indian languages. He now regularly works with Indian musicians to find a common musical language between the East and the West. He performed in the world's first Urdu opera "Sohini and Mahival" at London's Southbank Centre. He now regularly lends his operatic voice to music in Konkani and is also working with Konkani poets to create new music.
